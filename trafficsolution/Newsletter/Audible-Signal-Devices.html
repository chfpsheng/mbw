Audible Signal Devices Facilitate Pedestrians’ Lives

Accessible pedestrian signal (APS) is an electronic device working simultaneously with pedestrian light. It provides an audible signal to cross or stop for pedestrians, especially for the blind or visually impaired to ensure their safety and avoid delays when crossing the road.

This APS simulates the sound of metal percussion (clang) as a signal to pedestrians to cross the road. Frequency of the signal is for red light 70 hits per minute and green light 700 hits per minute.

It uses advanced digital voice module for recording and intelligent playback the record through the remote control. It can control the volume according to the status of the road junction to control the volume in different period or to stop playback. Stability, reliability, clear sound, low noise, flexible control, long life are the main advantages for this audible pedestrian signal device.
